Yeast of Yeetin'

Everything you might want or need to know about the wild and wonderful world of yeast!

If you want to make beautiful, tall baked goods with open crumbs and airy feels, you're gonna havr to get into leaveners. There are two primary options for leavening your baked goods: commercial yeast and sourdough starter.

Commercial yeast can be found easily at the supermarket or online. It comes in a granulated form and can have a mild, beer-like smell to it. The two most called for types are instant, which don't need to be activated, and active-dry, which must be "woken up" with some water prior to incorporating into dough.

Sourdough starter tales advantage of the wild yeasts and microbes that live on the surface of everything. These microbes consume sugar (and other stuff) and burp out gas. This gas is what gives breads their signature holes!
